ICON PROJECT


PROJECT DESCRIPTION

This was a project where I designed an app icon for a drone coffee delivery app for my graphic design class (IAT 102). The icon was done using adobe illustrator. The goals of this project was to learn how to convey meaning through images while considering the target audience. We were not allowed to use any words, letters or colours in the icon.

TIME FRAME

It took two weeks to complete this project. This included an iteration phase where the class received peer and instructor feedback on their designs.


INITIAL CHALLENGES

This was one of our early projects in this course and therefore I had no experience with design programs like Adobe Illustrator. It was quite the learning curve trying to figure out what features were available for me to use and how to use them. The next challenge was being limited to an app sized design. This meant that the design had to be very simple so that it could scale. It was difficult trying to simplify the drone as it is so intricate.

BRAINSTORMING PHASE

MIND MAP

In order to combine the attributes of coffee and a drone, I brainstormed ways I could represent the two separately using a mind map. When doing this, I tried to break down the drone into smaller parts. I also thought of things that were different but had the same meaning. For example flying could be represented with wings.

SKETCHES

The second prototype was iterated from the first prototype to make it capable with the features we were planning to implement. Specifically, we wanted to display the current memory of the system. My goal was to make sure everything fits in one screen to ensure ease of view while using the system. As a result, the load and start buttons were moved to the side of the screen. The addition of the step controls was also added during this iteration and needed buttons to get user input.

NARROWED DOWN DESIGNS

Out of these three, I proceeded to narrow it down to one using the feedback from my TA. I decided to go with the flying cup because the designs that used a whole drone was too detailed and not scalable. The composition of having a full drone made it not balanced since the drone was very big.

RESEARCH PHASE

Since the sketches were very high level designs, I wanted to look at more propeller options to figure out small details like if the propeller should be sharp or rounded. Below are the two propellers I explored and the vectorization of these images.

FINAL APP ICON

The process from turning the sketch into a vector resulted in some changes. The cup was tilted in the vector to portray more of a motion. The propeller was also changed into a helicopter style propeller to make it more clear as to what it is. The propeller from the initial sketch could be easily mistakened as a flower or tie. The sharp corners of the propeller suited the sharp corners of the cup as well. A rounded square was added to the background to give the icon more dimension. The idea was that it made the cup feel like it is flying towards you.

The colour of the icon went through quite a few iterations. At first, I went with a traditional approach and made the cup white. However, this meant the background had to be dark. I decided to go with a lighter background because people often start their day with coffee. But the dark background would be associated to a more night time environment.

REFLECTION

The project was successful. During my class critique session, my peers and instructors were able to understand the purpose of the application just by looking at the icon. The app icon was also scalable, which was something I initially struggled with in the beginning.

Through this project, I was able to learn how to use images to convey meaning. It took a lot of sketches and iterations in order to get this right. I learned the power of iteration and that your first idea is usually not your best one. I also was able to get a firm understanding of how to use Adobe Illustrator just by using the program and occasionally googling things I was unsure about. I can now apply my knowledge of Illustrator to other projects.

An improvement that I would change would be making the line that connects the propeller to the cup a lot more darker. The contrast between the background and this line is not different enough. Therefore, as you scale the icon down, this line disappears.
